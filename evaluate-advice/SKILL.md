# evaluate-advice

**Purpose**  
Help early-stage founders cut through noise and conflicting advice by providing a structured way to evaluate recommendations and choose the highest-signal path.

**When to use this skill**  
- Different people are giving you opposite or conflicting advice
- You're unsure which direction to take (e.g. raise now vs build more)
- You want to make decisions based on high-signal patterns rather than opinions

**Core Principles**  
- When advice conflicts, default to patterns that have worked for founders who actually shipped and raised.
- Advice that pushes you toward real customer evidence is usually higher signal.
- Advice that encourages raising money before you have traction is often lower signal.
- Generic advice is usually low value.

**Decision Framework**  
When evaluating conflicting advice, ask:

1. What stage is this advice optimized for?
2. What does this advice optimize for? (Raising money vs building a real business)
3. Does this match patterns from founders who succeeded?

**Action Steps**  
1. Clearly write down the conflicting pieces of advice.
2. Run them through the Decision Framework.
3. Decide on your next action based on the highest-signal option.

**What Good Looks Like**  
You feel clearer and can explain why you're choosing one path over another.

**Red Flags & Common Mistakes**  
- Following advice just because it came from someone impressive.
- Taking conflicting advice too personally instead of evaluating it objectively.

**Example Output**  
You can clearly explain your decision using real founder patterns instead of just repeating what someone told you.
