# validate-idea

**Purpose**  
Help early-stage founders avoid building something nobody wants by creating a structured process to validate real customer pain before committing to development.

**When to use this skill**  
- You have an idea but haven’t done serious customer discovery yet
- You're getting conflicting advice about whether the idea is good
- You feel pressure to start building quickly

**Core Principles**  
- You are not smarter than your potential customers.
- Talk to strangers who have no reason to be nice to you.
- Real evidence compounds. Vague positive feedback does not.
- Most founders overestimate how painful their problem is to others.

**Decision Framework**  
Before moving forward, answer:

1. Can you describe the target customer in one sentence?
2. What is their current workaround and how painful is it?
3. Have you spoken to at least 10–15 real potential customers?
4. Do you have clear evidence that some would use or pay for a solution?

**Action Steps**  
1. Write down your top 3 assumptions about the customer and problem.
2. Talk to real potential users (start with warm intros).
3. Focus on understanding their current pain, not pitching your idea.
4. Only move to building when you have repeatable evidence of pain + willingness to engage.

**What Good Looks Like**  
You have specific quotes and patterns from real customers. You feel slightly less excited about the original idea because you now understand the real problem better.

**Red Flags & Common Mistakes**  
- Only validating with friends and family.
- Building before speaking to real strangers.
- Treating “That’s interesting” as validation.

**Example Output**  
You can clearly explain the problem, current workarounds, and level of pain with real customer evidence.
