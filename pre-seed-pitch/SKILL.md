# pre-seed-pitch

**Purpose**  
Help early-stage founders approach investor conversations with clarity and focus on what actually matters at the pre-seed/seed stage.

**When to use this skill**  
- Preparing for investor meetings or accelerator interviews
- Getting conflicting feedback from different investors
- Unsure what to prioritize in your pitch or conversations

**Core Principles**  
- At pre-seed, investors mainly invest in **you**.
- Real customer evidence beats polished slides.
- “Why Now? Why Us?” are the two most important questions.
- Warm intros from other founders work much better than cold outreach.

**Decision Framework**  
Before any investor conversation, ask:

1. Do I have real customer evidence?
2. Can I clearly explain why this problem matters to me?
3. Do I understand why previous attempts failed?

**Action Steps**  
1. Gather real customer signals before heavy pitching.
2. Prepare a simple story: Why you + Why this problem + Early evidence.
3. Practice showing (demo) instead of just telling.
4. Be ready to answer “Why Now?” and “Why Us?”.

**What Good Looks Like**  
You enter investor conversations with calm confidence because you have real evidence and a clear story.

**Red Flags & Common Mistakes**  
- Spending months perfecting slides instead of talking to customers.
- Always being in “fundraising mode” instead of building mode.
- Taking conflicting investor advice too literally.

**Example Output**  
You can clearly explain your traction and story instead of just having a generic pitch deck.
